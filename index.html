Runna and Garmin both push every workout to Strava automatically, so this one connection covers all three. You need your own (free) Strava API app — a 5-minute, one-time setup:

  1. Open strava.com/settings/api and create an app (any name, category "Data Importer").

  3. Copy the Client ID and Client Secret below, then press Connect.

Apple Health — sleep, HRV, resting HR, VO₂max, weight

Apple doesn't let any web app read Health data live, so top this up occasionally (monthly is plenty — the app keeps everything you've already imported). On your iPhone:

  1. Health app → your picture (top right) → Export All Health Data.
  2. Save the zip to Files, long-press it → Uncompress.
  3. Pick the export.xml inside the folder below. Big files are fine — it's read in chunks.

Runna — upcoming plan

Runna publishes your training plan as a live calendar feed. Connect it and your future workouts appear in the Coach tab with prep guidance for each session:

  1. Runna app → Profile → Connected Apps & Devices → Calendars → Other Calendar.
  2. Copy the iCalendar link and paste it below, or open the link in a browser and import the downloaded .ics file.

Garmin — merged through Apple Health

Garmin blocks personal apps from pulling data directly, but it pushes everything into Apple Health, which merges your two watches automatically. One-time setup in Garmin Connect: Settings → Connected Apps → Apple Health → allow steps, heart rate, workouts, and sleep; and Settings → Connected Apps → Strava for runs. From then on: Garmin-watch nights and Apple-Watch nights both land in Apple Health (this app keeps whichever device recorded the fuller night — never double-counted), and all runs arrive live via Strava. Runs recorded only on Apple Watch are also picked out of the Health export and de-duplicated against Strava. One gap outside anyone's control: Garmin doesn't share HRV with Apple Health, so HRV may be blank on Garmin days — the readiness score reweights around it automatically.
